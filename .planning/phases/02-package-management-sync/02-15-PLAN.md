<objective>
Remove the cross-manager review coordinator and give every package job its own batched review inside its own `execute()`, per the corrected D-24.

Purpose: `PackagePhaseCoordinator` was built to satisfy a reading of D-24 the user never asked for. The corrected D-24 says the batching is per manager: the jobs are independent by D-15, and a coordinator that reviews every enabled manager at once contradicts that independence. Removing it deletes a component, a module, and 457 lines of tests for architecture nobody wanted, and it restores the plain `SyncJob` contract the orchestrator already knows how to run.

Output: `package_phase.py` and its tests deleted; a `Reviewer` protocol with a `TerminalUIReviewer` implementation injected through `JobContext`; `PackageSyncJob.execute()` performing plan, review, apply; the orchestrator's `_execute_jobs` reduced back to its plain job loop.
</objective>

<threat_model>
## Trust Boundaries

| Boundary | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| review decision to target mutation | An approved diff becomes a privileged command on the target; a review that is skipped or bypassed mutates a machine nobody inspected |
| orchestrator to job | The orchestrator supplies the reviewer; a job constructed without one must not degrade into applying unreviewed diffs |

| Threat ID | Category | Component | Severity | Disposition | Mitigation Plan |
|-----------|----------|-----------|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| T-02-38 | Elevation of Privilege | PackageSyncJob.execute | high | mitigate | `execute()` asserts a reviewer is present before planning; the missing-reviewer unit test proves no converge command is issued on that path |
| T-02-39 | Tampering | PackageSyncJob.apply | high | mitigate | `apply()` still converges only diffs carrying an APPLY decision from the outcome the job's own review returned; no fallback path constructs decisions |
| T-02-40 | Denial of Service | orchestrator job TaskGroup | medium | accept | The review prompt blocks the job loop while awaiting a human, which is the intended interaction; the disk-space monitors continue and the Live display is paused rather than contended |
