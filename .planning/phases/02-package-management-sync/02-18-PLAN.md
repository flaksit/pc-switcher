<objective>
Move the snippet registry's transport off `config_sync` and onto `manual_installs_sync` itself (D-23). Today the registry rides `config_sync`'s `SYNCED_CONFIG_FILENAMES` tuple alongside `config.yaml`, but `config_sync` runs at sync step 9 — before any review — so it cannot carry a snippet the user has not authored yet. The corrected D-23 has `manual_installs_sync` push `package-snippets.yaml` to the target with `send_file()` immediately after its own review, so on-the-fly snippets are included, and reverts `config_sync` to `config.yaml` only.

Purpose: the two halves are the same change and must land together. Making `manual_installs_sync` push the file itself, and only then removing `config_sync`'s carriage of it, guarantees there is never a window where an authored snippet fails to reach the target. It also removes a correctness dependency on another job: `config_sync` and `folder_sync` are surfaces the user controls and can disable, and no job's correctness may depend on another running.

Output: a snippet push in `manual_installs_sync` between its review and its snippet replay; `config_sync` reverted to a single-file `config.yaml` transfer with `SYNCED_CONFIG_FILENAMES` removed; every caller, docstring and test that referenced the tuple reverted with it.
</objective>
