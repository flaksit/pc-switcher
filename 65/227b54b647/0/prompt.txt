### (c) Definitive root cause

Not host-key drift. The failure is a `known_hosts` **content overwrite** by pc-switcher's own forward sync. `folder_sync` mirrors `/home` with rsync `--delete`. A forward sync pc1→pc2 therefore replaces pc2's `~/.ssh/known_hosts` with pc1's copy. Because pc1's `known_hosts` contains only a `pc2` entry and **no `pc1` self-entry**, pc2 ends up with a `known_hosts` that has zero entries for pc1. The subsequent reverse sync pc2→pc1 then fails with exactly `No ED25519 host key is known for pc1` — the error the maintainer saw. This is precisely the mechanism the codebase already documents (`test_end_to_end_sync.py:244` "`.ssh/known_hosts` is excluded because…", and `:570` "clobbered by the --delete mirror of /home").

The two hypotheses from my first pass are **disproven** by the evidence above: no single-VM reprovision (`rebuilt: null`, shared creation time), no host-key regeneration (mtimes = provisioning time), no baseline drift (both baselines match running keys).
